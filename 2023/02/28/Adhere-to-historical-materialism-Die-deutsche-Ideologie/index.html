Background

Immediate cause: Criticism of Young Hegelian philosophy and â€œtrue socialismâ€.

  1. The Young Hegelians are the radical Hegelian Left. The main feature is religious criticism. Philosophy is speculative philosophy.
    They believed that the problem in Germany at that time was not a problem of actual social change, but mainly a problem of backward thinking. So their views are only reflected in their thoughts, not in their actions. So Marx believed that they seemed radical, but they were actually the most conservative.
  2. The Hegelian obligatory school is the old school/systematic school, and the place of activity is in Berlin.
  3. True socialism: A trend of thought that arose in the German intellectual circles at that time. These socialists gave their ideas a real name, which was very arrogant. This reflected the hyperbole and arrogance of the German ideological circle at that time, believing that only German science could give socialism a solid foundation. They are keen on theoretical speculation, and their theoretical basis comes from the Young Hegelians.
  4. What they all have in common is that they stay at using words to refute some words and sentences, and they stay at the internal struggle of ideology. In order to realize the healthy development of German society, it is objectively necessary to criticize these ideologies.

Clarify the philosophical insights of Marx and Engels themselves.

Both had participated in the Young Hegelian movement. Later parted ways. 1845 sought a theoretical self-clarification by liquidating previous philosophical beliefs. At the same time, it clarifies the new worldview formed at that time.
